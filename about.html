About Us

Learn about our parish history, mission, and leadership

Parish History

Queen of Apostles Parish has a rich history of faith, community, and service. Explore our journey through the years.

1965

Queen of Apostles Parish was established as a mission outpost by the Consolata Missionaries.

1972

The first church building was constructed and dedicated to Our Lady, Queen of Apostles.

1985

The parish expanded with the establishment of the first outstation in Marurui.

1998

St. Gabriel outstation was established to serve the growing community.

2010

The main church building was renovated and expanded to accommodate the growing congregation.

2023

Today, Queen of Apostles Parish continues to grow as a vibrant community of faith, serving thousands of parishioners across multiple outstations.

Our Patron Saint

Mary, Queen of Apostles

Mary, Queen of Apostles, is the patroness of our parish. After the Ascension of Jesus, Mary remained with the Apostles in prayer and supported them as they began their mission to spread the Gospel. As Queen of Apostles, Mary continues to guide and intercede for the Church in its mission of evangelization.

Our parish is dedicated to Mary under this title to emphasize our commitment to the apostolic mission of the Church and to seek her maternal guidance in our efforts to spread the Good News of Jesus Christ.

Our Clergy Team

Meet the dedicated priests who serve our parish community.

Fr. James Mwangi

Parish Priest

Fr. James has been serving as the Parish Priest of Queen of Apostles since 2015. He was ordained in 1998 and has dedicated his priesthood to building vibrant parish communities. He has a special interest in youth ministry and liturgical formation.

Fr. Peter Kamau

Assistant Priest

Fr. Peter joined our parish in 2020. He was ordained in 2012 and has served in various parishes across the Archdiocese. He oversees the sacramental preparation programs and is passionate about adult faith formation.

Dcn. Joseph Njoroge

Permanent Deacon

Deacon Joseph was ordained to the permanent diaconate in 2018. He assists with liturgical celebrations, baptisms, and marriage preparation. He also coordinates the parish's outreach to the sick and homebound.
