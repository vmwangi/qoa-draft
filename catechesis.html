Our Catechesis Philosophy

Rooted in the teachings of the Catholic Church, our catechesis programs aim to form lifelong disciples through a Christ-centered, sacramental, and community-oriented approach.

  • Christ-Centered: Emphasizes a personal relationship with Jesus Christ, grounded in Scripture and Tradition.

  • Sacramental Focus: Prepares participants to encounter Christ in the sacraments, particularly Reconciliation, Eucharist, and Confirmation.

  • Community-Oriented: Encourages active participation in the parish community, fostering a sense of belonging.

  • Lifelong Faith Formation: Recognizes catechesis as a continuous journey, supporting parishioners at every stage of life.

Children's Catechism (Ages 4–12)

The Children's Catechism program introduces young children to the Catholic faith and prepares them for First Reconciliation and First Holy Communion. Running from September to May, it fosters spiritual growth through engaging and age-appropriate methods.

Program Details

  • Weekly Saturday classes (9:00 AM–11:00 AM), divided by age groups (4–6, 7–9, 10–12).
  • Curriculum based on resources like Alive in Christ, covering the Trinity, sacraments, prayer, and moral life.
  • Sacramental preparation for Grade 2 students (ages 7–8), with First Reconciliation and First Holy Communion celebrated in spring.
  • Activities include storytelling, crafts, songs, and multimedia to engage young learners.

Family Involvement

  • Monthly family catechesis sessions to reinforce lessons at home.
  • Parents receive prayer guides and activity books to support their role as primary educators.

Additional Information

  • Led by trained catechists under the Director of Religious Education.
  • Registration requires baptismal certificates for sacramental preparation; modest fee with financial assistance available.
  • Children participate in parish events like Advent and Lenten activities.

Contact the parish office at +254 123 456 789 or info@queenofapostles.org for more details or to register.

Youth Confirmation (Ages 13–17)

The Youth Confirmation program is a two-year journey preparing teens for the Sacrament of Confirmation, empowering them to live as active members of the Church through a deepened relationship with the Holy Spirit.

Program Details

  • Weekly Sunday sessions (2:00 PM–4:00 PM), using resources like Chosen or Decision Point.
  • Covers the Holy Spirit, Church history, apologetics, and Christian living, addressing teen-relevant issues.
  • Includes a weekend retreat, service projects, and mentorship from Confirmation sponsors.
  • Confirmation Mass celebrated annually, often with the bishop presiding.

Teaching Methods

  • Facilitated by youth ministers and catechists, combining large-group teaching, small-group discussions, and peer-led activities.
  • Incorporates contemporary media, such as videos and social media challenges.

Additional Information

  • Open to teens who have received First Communion; late starters may join with catch-up sessions.
  • Teens join the parish youth group for social events and mission trips.
  • Parents attend an orientation and occasional sessions.

Contact the parish office at +254 123 456 789 or info@queenofapostles.org for more details or to register.

Adult Faith Formation

The Adult Faith Formation program supports adults in deepening their faith through the Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults (RCIA) for those entering the Church and ongoing formation for practicing Catholics.

RCIA Details

  • Weekly Wednesday sessions (6:30 PM–8:00 PM) for catechumens and candidates.
  • Follows RCIA stages: Inquiry, Catechumenate, Purification and Enlightenment, and Mystagogy.
  • Covers Scripture, the Creed, sacraments, and morality; culminates in sacraments at the Easter Vigil.

Ongoing Formation Details

  • Thursday sessions (7:00 PM–8:30 PM), using resources like The Bible Timeline or Symbolon.
  • Monthly or biweekly topics include Scripture study, Church history, and Vatican II teachings.
  • Guest speakers and small faith-sharing groups enhance learning.

Additional Information

  • RCIA participants are paired with sponsors; ongoing formation is flexible with drop-in attendance.
  • Encourages involvement in parish ministries and seasonal retreats.
  • Led by the parish priest, deacon, or trained lay catechists.

Contact the parish office at +254 123 456 789 or info@queenofapostles.org for more details or to register.
