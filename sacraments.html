Sacraments

The seven sacred signs of grace instituted by Christ

Sacrament Guide

The seven sacraments are signs of grace instituted by Christ to give his divine life to us through the work of the Holy Spirit. Learn about each sacrament, its requirements, and how to receive it at Queen of Apostles Parish.

Anointing of the Sick

The Anointing of the Sick is a sacrament of healing that provides spiritual and sometimes physical strength during illness, especially near the time of death.

Requirements:

  • The person should be baptized Catholic
  • The person should be seriously ill, facing surgery, or weakened by old age
  • The sacrament can be received multiple times if the illness worsens

For emergency situations, please call the parish office immediately at any time.

Baptism

Baptism is the first sacrament of Christian initiation, freeing us from original sin and making us members of the Church, Christ's body.

Requirements:

  • Completed registration form
  • Birth certificate of the child (for infant baptism)
  • Parents must be registered parishioners
  • Godparents must be practicing Catholics who have received Confirmation
  • Parents and godparents must attend pre-baptismal classes
Download Baptism Registration Form

First Holy Communion

First Holy Communion is the reception of the Eucharist for the first time, usually during childhood, where we receive the Body and Blood of Christ.

Requirements:

  • Child must be baptized Catholic
  • Child must be at least 7 years old
  • Completion of two years of religious education
  • Regular attendance at Sunday Mass
  • Baptismal certificate must be submitted
Download First Communion Registration Form

Confirmation

Confirmation completes the grace of Baptism by a special outpouring of the Holy Spirit, which seals and strengthens us as witnesses of Christ.

Requirements:

  • Must be baptized Catholic
  • Must have received First Holy Communion
  • Must be at least 14 years old
  • Completion of two years of Confirmation preparation
  • Regular attendance at Sunday Mass
  • Selection of a Confirmation sponsor who is a practicing Catholic
  • Selection of a Confirmation name (saint's name)
Download Confirmation Registration Form

Reconciliation

The Sacrament of Reconciliation (Confession) offers forgiveness of sins committed after Baptism, reconciling us with God and the Church.

Regular Confession Times:

  • Saturdays: 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM
  • Thursdays: 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM
  • First Fridays: 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM
  • By appointment (contact the parish office)

First Reconciliation (Children):

  • Child must be baptized Catholic
  • Child must be at least 7 years old
  • Completion of religious education classes

Matrimony

The Sacrament of Matrimony is a covenant by which a man and a woman establish a partnership for life, ordered to the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of children.

Requirements:

  • At least one party must be a baptized Catholic
  • Both parties must be free to marry in the Catholic Church
  • Couples must contact the parish at least 6 months before the desired wedding date
  • Completion of pre-marriage preparation program
  • Recent baptismal certificates (issued within 6 months)
  • Completion of pre-nuptial investigation
  • Marriage license from civil authorities
Download Marriage Preparation Checklist

Holy Orders

Holy Orders is the sacrament through which the mission entrusted by Christ to his apostles continues to be exercised in the Church until the end of time. It includes three degrees: episcopate (bishops), presbyterate (priests), and diaconate (deacons).

Requirements:

  • Must be a baptized, confirmed Catholic male in good standing
  • Must have a genuine call to serve God and His people
  • Must complete required education and formation
  • For priesthood: typically requires a bachelor's degree and 4-5 years of seminary
  • For permanent diaconate: typically requires 4-5 years of formation

If you feel called to the priesthood or diaconate, please speak with our parish priest for guidance and discernment.
